5. Key Segment: Monthly Trial

7. What Are We Showing Users Currently?

67% of new Plus users have no RFMC segment. The recommendation engine relies on RFMC to personalize content. Users without RFMC get a generic fallback experience, which correlates with lower engagement and retention.

With RFMC (33% of users) — what they see
WhenWhat they get
Day 0 Same hardcoded curated list (RFMC is ignored on day 0).
Days 1–7 (RFMC 1–2) Same as no-RFMC: 5 shuffled from top-10 + 15 from top-25 (3-KPI rank). Locked positions apply.
Days 1–7 (RFMC 3–5) Top 35 by 4 KPIs, shuffled. Disney injected at positions 0-2. Daily rotation for RFMC 5.
Fallback: If CO produces ≤12 activities → tries 0d lifecycle segment recs, otherwise falls back to a hardcoded 20-game list.
Content repetition — how stale is the first week?

76% of users have their most-repeated activity appear on 2+ different days. 55% on 3+ days, 40% on 4+ days. Only 24% see unique content each day. The limited pool size (20–35 activities) combined with shuffling (not rotation) makes repetition inevitable.

Source: multifeed_shown_content · top-5 positions · feed 1 only · n=37,455 users · per-user max repeat distribution · W0 window. Long tail extends to 142 days. Day 0 hardcoded lists are not captured here.
8. Next Steps
#ActionWhy
1 Fix content recommendation for new users.
84% of Monthly Trial users are onboarding with no RFMC — they get a generic hardcoded list. Improving Day 0–7 content surfacing (cold-start personalization, better fallback logic) directly impacts trial engagement, which is the strongest predictor of retention.		 Foundational.
Affects activation, trial engagement, and W0 re-engagement simultaneously.
2 Set activation thresholds and track performance.
Use Section 6 targets (days / games / unique) as KPIs. Monitor segment-level progress via UX improvements, CRM nudges, and in-app messaging.		 Turns this analysis into an ongoing performance framework, not a one-off report.
Content rec is the multiplier. Every other lever (CRM, UX, IAM) works better when the content experience matches the user. Fix the foundation first, then amplify with nudges.
