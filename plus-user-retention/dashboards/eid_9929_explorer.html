1. Executive Summary

EID 9929 — Multifeed variety scaling for W0 Plus users. Control = ~17 fixed activities (manually injected) | Variant B = 23–38 activities based on ranking algorithm (injection removed). Mar 11+, 2026. Data as of Mar 30. Cancel metric: auto_renew_disabled (synced with GrowthBook).

Bottom line (Mar 30, GrowthBook-synced): Mixed result with a real tradeoff. Monthly cancel: VarB significantly better (10.3% → 9.1%, GrowthBook CTW 99.3%, −11.4%). Yearly cancel: VarB worse (10.4% → 11.4%, GrowthBook CTW 3.6% = 96.4% prob of harm, +9.7%). Both treatment arms hurt yearly (WithInj +8.7%, NOinj +9.7%). Revenue sizing: monthly savings ×3 ≈ yearly harm — roughly a wash. Needs stakeholder decision.

8. Deep Dive: Anchor Activity Displacement

Why high-converting anchor games lost impressions and PTR in Variant B.

Case study — Disney Frozen Anna: PTR dropped from 106.5% to 66.0% (−40.5pp). Root cause: In Control, Anna is concentrated at positions 1–4 (89% of impressions) where PTR is high. 10. Sticker vs Bathroom Game Deep Dive

Both are top-played “activities” from multifeed — but they are fundamentally different experiences.

Stickers are not a game. They’re a reward collection mechanism: median 13s session, 0% completion rate, no gameplay loop. Bathroom Game is a real game: 84s median, ~70% completion. Treating them the same in rankings is misleading.

12. Trial vs Direct Payment Analysis

80–92% of experiment users are active trial users who haven’t paid yet. This is the dominant confounding factor.

Active trial users drive the negative signal. Monthly active trial users in VarB play 19% fewer MF games (16.7 vs 20.6) while direct payment users are flat (19.7 vs 19.8). Yearly active trial users also show a 6.5% drop in VarB. Direct payment users are unaffected by the experiment across all cuts.

13. Most Replayed Activities

Which activities do users come back to? Replay = same user playing same activity multiple times. High replay = sticky content.

Bathroom game is the replay king — 1,455 users in Control choose it as their most-replayed activity (avg 6.4 replays). In VarB, bathroom drops to 1,157 users and 4.8 replays (−25% replay volume). Stickers rise to #1 by user count in VarB (629 vs 570). More variety fragments replay behavior.

Takeaway
  • VarB fragments replay behavior: bathroom loses 298 users (−20%) and 1.6 avg replays (−25%). The #1 engagement anchor is diluted
  • Disney runners nearly disappear as top-replayed activities: frozen_kingdom drops from 233 to 88 users (−62%)
  • Uniquebuilder games (pastry, cat, pizza, dentist, meal) all gain users in VarB — but at lower replay rates (2.8–3.6 vs 3.5–4.6)
  • Stickers: more users (629 vs 570) but they’re a “dead end” (0% completion, 13s median duration) — replays of a non-game inflate the number
  • Net effect: variety redistributes engagement across more activities but reduces total engagement volume per user
