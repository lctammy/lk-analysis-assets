2.1 Daily Cancellations — 1,805/Day We Can Act On

From the pool, 1,805 new cancellations happen each day (Mar 9-25 avg, post-dedup). 60% monthly, 40% yearly. Split by plan, frequency, engagement, and timing.

45% of daily cancels are repeat cancellers (2+ lifetime cancels, 819/day). They're active players (31 games/7d). First-time cancellers = 54% but less predictable.
Inactivity is the strongest predictor of cancellation. Monthly: 2.2× cancel gap (4.45% inactive vs 2.04% heavy). Yearly: 5× gap (0.30% vs 0.06%). More play = monotonically lower cancel rate.

⚠ Revenue estimate caveats:
1. Per-event, not per-user. 45% are repeat cancellers (median gap 68d). If we save 85 repeat monthly users today, they cancel again in ~68 days — we "save" them again. Annual save counts are inflated by repeat events on same users.
2. Monthly: 1 save ≈ 1 extra month, not 3. Cancel = disable auto-renewal. Saving re-enables it for next billing cycle. Most repeat cancellers re-cancel within 1-2 months.
3. Yearly: 1 save ≈ 1 renewal, but timing matters. A yearly user canceling on day 30 of their year — renewal is 335 days away. They can re-cancel any time before that. Revenue is only realized at renewal.
Conservative estimate: 1 additional payment per save event × 10% save rate × 1,805/day × 365 = ~$2.2M/yr gross. Net after cannibalization and repeat inflation likely lower — Section 2.3 model is best reference.

Logic: Revenue if saved = window × price × (1-discount). Cannibalization = P(natural return within window) × window/2 × full price (avg returner comes back at midpoint). Net = saved revenue - cannibalized revenue.

Repeat cancellers are the WORST discount target. 6th+ monthly: 29% return within 3 months naturally. Offering 3-month discounts cannibalizes ~29% of the value.
Best discount targets: first-time yearly (480/day). Only 2.3% return within 3 months → 97.7% incremental.
Strategy:
  • Repeat (819/day, 2+ cancels) → Don't discount. Ask "What would keep you?" + yearly commitment offer.
  • First-time yearly (480/day) → Discount aggressively (3-6mo). Lowest cannibalization, highest price/save.
  • First-time monthly (533/day) → Short discount (1-3mo) or upsell to yearly.
